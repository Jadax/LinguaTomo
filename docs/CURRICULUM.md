# Curriculum and pedagogy

## Progression contract

The route combines, but does not conflate:

- FSI-style practical situations and conversation management
- CEFR and JF Standard Can-Do statements
- JLPT knowledge and exam-readiness references
- ILR functional proficiency references

The long-term functional target is ILR 3. Passing JLPT N1 alone does not prove
ILR 3 speaking or professional interaction.

LinguaTomo provides the complete instructional route from first kana to
advanced independent use. It has no instructor marketplace and does not imply
access to Japanese teachers. It can also act as a structured companion for
learners who choose to work with an external teacher.

## Current structure

| Stage | Reference | Functional focus |
|---|---|---|
| Kitten Steps | Pre-N5, Pre-A1, ILR 0 | Kana, sound system and learning routines |
| First Encounters | N5, A1, ILR 0+ | Greetings, introductions and directions |
| Daily Life | N4, A2, ILR 1 | Transport, shopping, food, telephone and emergencies |
| Independent Japan | N3, B1, ILR 1+/2 | Narration, health, work and supported opinions |
| Connected Japan | N2, B2, ILR 2 | News, formal requests and negotiation |
| Professional Japan | N1+, C1, ILR 2+/3 | Complex texts, briefings and mediation |

For an absolute beginner, the route starts with Japanese sound distinctions and
hiragana. Useful greetings and interaction chunks begin in parallel, so the
learner can communicate before mastering every symbol. Katakana follows early;
kanji then grows through components, high-frequency words and contextual
reading. This dual strand matches the practical Can-Do emphasis of the Japan
Foundation’s Irodori materials while preserving the character foundation that
independent reading requires. Advanced learners can place out rather than being
forced through kana drills.

Current bundled scale:

- 18 assessed situational Can-Do missions
- 828 grammar reference lessons
- 30 postcards
- 24 seasonal story side quests
- 104 handwriting and photo-practice targets across both basic syllabaries and
  the currently bundled introductory kanji set

The bundled route is a tested foundation, not yet a complete expert curriculum.
Vocabulary, systematic kanji, graded reading, verified listening and original
exam banks remain tracked delivery work. The interface must state this honestly
until each coverage gate has passed.

## Lesson quality gate

A lesson must have:

1. a plain-English Can-Do or learning objective;
2. accurate Japanese reviewed against at least two reliable references;
3. context, register and culture notes where relevant;
4. at least one retrieval or production task;
5. accessible interaction without a mandatory timer;
6. a stable identifier and provenance record;
7. an explicit level label described as official or community-estimated;
8. tests for imported structure and counts.

## Assessment policy

- Placement is untimed and can skip familiar core missions.
- FSRS ratings describe recall, not worth or intelligence.
- Undo should be available before a response becomes irreversible.
- Speaking recognition must tolerate accents and show the transcript used.
- Handwriting scores must separate shape, stroke order, balance and confidence.
- Mock examinations must reproduce published exam structures without claiming
  official endorsement or copying protected questions.

## Content presentation

Use progressive disclosure:

1. beginner explanation;
2. pattern and examples;
3. nuance, register and exceptions;
4. trusted further reading;
5. practice in a real situation.

Grammar is best reinforced in context. Isolated kanji practice should connect
components to real vocabulary rather than requiring readings to be memorised as
detached lists.
