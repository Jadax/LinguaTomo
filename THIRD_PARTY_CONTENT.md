# Third-party content

LinguaTomo separates source code, learning data and outbound resource links. A
repository being open source does not automatically grant rights to every item
linked from it.

## Included learning data

### Hanabira Japanese grammar

- Source: <https://github.com/tristcoil/hanabira.org-japanese-content>
- Website: <https://hanabira.org>
- Source revision: `fb2d03f14e8000ef3c77612c7770e425c012c904`
- Licence stated by the publisher: Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 3.0
- Included files: the five proofread Japanese grammar JSON collections, N5 to
  N1, containing 828 grammar points in total
- Local path: `assets/content/grammar/`
- LinguaTomo changes: filenames normalised; stable local identifiers, search,
  progress and review metadata are added at runtime

Attribution must remain visible in the app and in redistributed builds. Any
adaptation of this corpus must be shared under compatible CC BY-SA terms.

## Deliberately excluded

- Sentence collections described as being derived from commercial textbooks
- Repositories or data without an explicit reusable licence
- Kana Dojo source code, which is AGPL-3.0 and is used only as product research
- Audio whose contributor-specific licence has not been verified
- Third-party logos, characters and screenshots

## Research directories

The Awesome Japanese and donkuri repositories are useful curated directories,
but their licences cover their lists rather than every linked resource. They
inform the resource audit and do not provide blanket permission to copy linked
material.
